Biography



APJ Abdul Kalam was an Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, to a Tamil Muslim family.

Kalam completed his education at St. Joseph's College in Tiruchirappalli, Tamil Nadu, and went on to study aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology. After graduating in 1960, he joined the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) as a scientist.

During his time at DRDO, Kalam played a key role in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle and was later appointed as the Chief Executive of the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program. He also served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the Defense Research and Development Organization.

Kalam was awarded the Padma Bhushan in 1981 and the Padma Vibhushan in 1990 for his contributions to science and engineering. He was also honored with the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 1997.

In 2002, Kalam was elected as the President of India, succeeding K.R. Narayanan. During his presidency, Kalam focused on education, youth development, and national unity. He was known for his simplicity, humility, and commitment to public service.

After his presidency, Kalam continued to be actively involved in education and social causes. He was a visiting professor at several universities and worked to promote scientific research and entrepreneurship among Indian youth.

Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. He was posthumously awarded the Bharat Ratna in 2015, making him the first President of India to receive the honor after his death.





Achivements



APJ Abdul Kalam was a renowned scientist and served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He made significant contributions in the field of science and technology, especially in the areas of aeronautics and defense. Some of his notable achievements are:

1.Played a key role in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III and the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) which made India a member of the space club.

2.Led the development of the nuclear-capable ballistic missile Agni and guided missile program.

3.Served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister of India and played a key role in the country's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.

4.Received several awards and honors for his contributions to science and technology, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 1997.

5.Was an author and wrote several books on his experiences and ideas, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds".

6.Was a popular and inspiring teacher and mentor to young people, promoting science education and encouraging innovation and creativity.
